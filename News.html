What is Environmental Sustainability?



Environmental sustainability refers to the responsible interaction with the environment to avoid the depletion or degradation of natural resources, ensuring long-term ecological balance. It involves adopting practices that protect ecosystems, reduce pollution, conserve water and energy, and minimize waste. The goal of environmental sustainability is to meet the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs, fostering a harmonious relationship between human activities and the natural world.

Global Warming and Weather Predictions: Due to climate change, traditional forecasting models for phenomena like El Niño and La Niña have become less reliable. Meteorologists now rely more on real-time data, like ocean temperatures and greenhouse gas levels, to predict these events accurately​( World Economic Forum )

Carbon Capture Expansion


A new study emphasizes that expanding carbon capture and storage technology is critical to keeping global warming within the 2°C limit set by the Paris Climate Agreement. Without significant investment, this technology may not advance quickly enough to meet climate targets​( ScienceDaily ).

Microplastics


Microplastics and Soil Pollution: Research shows that soil contamination from pesticides, heavy metals, and microplastics poses risks to cardiovascular health, further highlighting the need for improved environmental policies to tackle pollution​( ScienceDaily ).
